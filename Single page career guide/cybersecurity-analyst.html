What you'll do

Real work.
Real impact.

In South Africa, analysts protect banks, hospitals, municipalities, and millions of people's personal data. This work matters here in a way that's impossible to overstate.

👁️

Monitoring & Detection

Watch the walls. You track network traffic, parse logs, and use SIEM platforms to catch suspicious behaviour before it turns into a breach. South Africa's banks and telcos run 24/7 SOCs that depend on analysts like you.

SIEMSplunkSentinelSOC
🚨

Incident Response

When something breaks, you don't panic - you lead. Contain the damage, trace the attacker, document everything, and help the organisation recover stronger. SA has seen major breaches at Transnet, SARS, and Experian - IR analysts are the ones who fix it.

ForensicsPlaybooksTriage
🔭

Threat Intelligence

Understand the adversary. You research threat actor groups targeting SA financial institutions and government, map their behaviour using MITRE ATT&CK, and brief the team on what's coming next.

MITRE ATT&CKIOCsThreat Feeds
🩺

Vulnerability Management

Find the cracks before attackers do. You scan systems, rank risks using CVSS, and work with developers and sysadmins to close gaps - a constant and critical cycle.

NessusQualysCVE
📜

Compliance & POPIA

South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) makes compliance a legal and ethical necessity. You ensure the organisation handles data lawfully, documents controls, and stays audit-ready.

POPIAISO 27001NISTGDPR
🤝

Security Awareness

Teach people to protect themselves. In a country where social engineering and phishing attacks are rampant, building human defences through training and simulations is one of the highest-leverage things you'll do.

TrainingPhishing SimsCulture

Where you'll work

SA industries that
need you most.

Cybersecurity roles exist across every sector in South Africa - from Sandton's financial district to government departments in Pretoria.

🏦

Banking & Finance

Standard Bank, Absa, Nedbank, FNB, and Capitec all run large internal SOC teams. The highest salaries in SA cyber live here.

📡

Telecoms

MTN, Vodacom, and Telkom protect massive subscriber networks and are frequent targets for SIM-swap and infrastructure attacks.

🏛️

Government & SAPS

SAPS, SARS, Home Affairs, and the Department of Defence all have cybersecurity needs - and SITA coordinates government-wide security.

🏥

Healthcare

Netcare, Mediclinic, and Discovery Health hold sensitive patient data. Healthcare breaches in SA are rising fast.

⛏️

Mining & Energy

Anglo American, Sasol, and Eskom operate critical infrastructure. OT/ICS security is a growing specialisation here.

🛡️

MSSPs & Consulting

Companies like Liquid Cyber, Performanta, and BCX Security provide managed security services across Africa - great launchpad roles.

Career path

Your journey
in South Africa.

There's no single route in. Many of SA's best analysts started in IT support, call centres, or completely different fields. What matters is the drive to learn.

Phase 1 · 0-2 years

Build the foundation

Start with IT support, helpdesk, or networking at a company like BCX, Dimension Data, or a corporate IT team. Earn your first certs and get your hands dirty in home labs. Platforms like TryHackMe work from any Mzansi Wi-Fi connection.

CompTIA A+ Network+ Security+ Google Cyber Cert
Phase 2 · 2-5 years

Specialise & go deeper

You're in a SOC now - maybe at a bank, an MSSP, or a government agency. You're handling real incidents, writing detection rules, and building the instincts that only experience gives you. POPIA knowledge sets you apart locally.

CySA+ CEH GCIA SC-200
Phase 3 · 5-10 years

Lead & shape strategy

Senior roles at major SA banks, Naspers, MTN, or large consulting firms. You're owning programs, mentoring juniors, and presenting risk to executives. Some go red team; some build out threat intel practices.

CISSP OSCP CISM GREM
Phase 4 · 10+ years

Define the vision

CISO roles at JSE-listed companies, government departments, or pan-African organisations. You set strategy, brief boards, manage teams, and shape policy at a national level. South Africa needs more local CISOs urgently.

CISM CISA CGEIT

A word on getting started in SA

You don't need a degree to break in - but the University of Pretoria, Wits, UCT, and UNISA all offer relevant programmes. The CSIR and NCPF (National Cybersecurity Policy Framework) are shaping local standards. Bursaries from SITA and private sector companies exist. Start with free labs, earn Security+, and apply - the industry is hungry for good people.
